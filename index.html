Investment Philosophy

Six beliefs. Each one
demands implementation.

Read full framework
01

Trends Persist

Markets trend more than they mean-revert over medium horizons. Supported by Moskowitz, Ooi & Pedersen (2012). Never fight the weekly timeframe.

→ Supertrend, Darvas, momentum composites
02

Right-Tail Convexity

A few outsized winners drive returns. Accept sub-50% win rates in exchange for asymmetric payoffs — the mathematical core of trend-following.

→ Position sizing is the real edge
03

Rules Beat Discretion

Every strategy has codified entry, exit, and sizing rules. If you can't backtest it, you can't trade it.

→ All dashboards are 100% rules-based
04

Entry Point Discipline

Deploy after flat or negative stretches, not after strong runs. When trailing Sharpe > 1.5, wait. Flat or negative? Deploy.

→ Behavioural calibration, not timing
05

Survive All Regimes

Good strategies survive every regime. Overly regime-conditioned systems break when regimes shift. Time your capital, not the strategy.

→ Strategy-level DCA, not market timing
06

Multi-Timeframe Confirmation

Weekly sets direction, Daily gives the setup, 4H confirms timing, 1H executes. Require 3-of-4 alignment before any trade.

→ W → D → 4H → 1H framework
Universal Process

One framework,
every asset class

Whether trading gold, a Russell 2000 small-cap, USDJPY, or Bitcoin — the process is identical. Only the parameters adapt.

Explore the full framework
1
Thesis & Selection — Identify a structural or cyclical reason for directional movement. Fundamental screens answer “what to trade.”
2
Trend Confirmation — Apply the W → D → 4H → 1H framework. Thesis without price confirmation = no trade.
3
Breadth Validation — Is the move broad-based or isolated? Silver + gold + copper + DXY all confirming = high conviction.
4
Entry & Sizing — ATR-based sizing, max 2% account risk. Start at 50%, scale into winners.
5
Management — Trail with Daily Supertrend. Never manually tighten. Let winners run, cut losers fast.
